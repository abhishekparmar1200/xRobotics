Description

Here at X Robotics we are committed to solving one of the hardest problems in robotics foundational models: Memory.

Where VLAs struggle

  1. Non Markovian tasks
  2. Long horizon tasks
  3. Caching tasks that have already been performed and performing them better the next time.

Our answer

We propose a unified control and memory layer (Spatial RAG) for embodied agents that transforms VLAs from stateless planners into context-aware learners.

Our system integrates spatial memory, safety, navigation, and perception into a modular OS-like framework, enabling agents to cache and refine behaviors across executions. Preliminary results on long-horizon, non-Markovian benchmarks show consistent performance gains, suggesting a path toward reusable, persistent-memory architectures for autonomous robotics.

Results

We are using Behaviour1K as the benchmark to test our techniques. In total there are 52 high quality Long Horizon Tasks on which we have tested and achieved state of the art results.
